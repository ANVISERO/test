<p><strong>Total compensation costs</strong> for civilian workers increased 0.3 percent from December 2008 to March 2009, seasonally adjusted, the Bureau of Labor Statistics of the U.S. Department of Labor reported today.  This follows a 0.6 percent increase for the September to December 2008 period.
  </p>
<p>In March 2009, <strong>wages</strong> and <strong>salaries</strong> also rose 0.3 percent, while benefits rose 0.5 percent.  <strong>The Employment Cost Index (ECI)</strong>, a product of the National Compensation Survey, measures changes in compensation costs for civilian workers (nonfarm private industry and state and local government
    workers). </p>
<p>Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</p>
