 <p>Employers initiated 3,489 <strong>mass layoff events</strong> in the first quarter
  of 2009 that resulted in the separation of 558,909 workers from their
  jobs for at least 31 days, according to preliminary figures released
  by the U.S. Department of Labor's Bureau of Labor Statistics.  </p>
<p>Both
    the number of extended <strong>mass layoff events</strong> and associated separations
    reached their highest first quarter levels in program history (with
    data available back to 1996), and both measures more than doubled 
    from the first quarter of 2008.</p>
<p>Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</p>
