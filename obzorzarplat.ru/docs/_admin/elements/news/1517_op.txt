<p>In private industry, <strong>compensation costs</strong> rose 1.9 percent in the year ended March 2009, significantly less than the increase for the year ended March 2008, which was 3.2 percent.
<p align="center"><img src="http://www.bls.gov/opub/ted/images/2009/apr/wk4/art05.gif" width="580" height="360"></p>
<p><strong>Private industry wages</strong> and <strong>salaries</strong> decelerated to a 2.0-percent increase for the year ended March 2009. In March 2008, the increase in <strong>wages</strong> and <strong>salaries</strong> was 3.2 percent.</p>
<p><strong>Benefit costs</strong> increased 1.6 percent for the 12-month period ended March 2009. For the year ended March 2008, the increase in <strong>benefit costs</strong> was 3.2 percent.</p>
<p>Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics</p>
